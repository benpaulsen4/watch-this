The weekly schedule helps you build a consistent watch routine. Schedule a TV show on certain days, and WatchThis will surface reminders and one-click “next episode” actions on those days.

## Before You Start

You can only schedule TV shows that are:

- **Planning**
- **Watching**
- **Paused**

Shows marked **Completed** or **Dropped** can’t be scheduled.

Read: [Watch Status Explained](/help/tracking/watch-status-explained).

## Add a Show to Your Weekly Schedule

1. Open a TV show’s details view.
2. Set a watch status if you haven’t already (for example **Planning**).
3. Open the **Schedule** tab.
4. Click **Add** next to the day(s) you want.

![Schedule Tab](/help/tracking/schedule-tab.png)

## Remove a Scheduled Day

1. Open the show’s **Schedule** tab.
2. Click **Remove** next to the day you no longer want.

## What Scheduling Does (and Doesn’t Do)

- Scheduling is a weekly pattern. It doesn’t track specific calendar dates.
- You can schedule the same show on multiple days.
- You can schedule multiple shows on the same day.
- Scheduling doesn’t automatically mark anything watched; it just helps you stay on track.

## Use Scheduled Prompts to Keep Up

On scheduled days, WatchThis can show a “Have you watched this today?” prompt and let you mark the next episode watched.

Read: [Mark the Next Episode Watched](/help/tracking/mark-the-next-episode-watched).
