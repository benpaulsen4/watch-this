WatchThis currently generates recommendations in the context of a list. When you open a list, you may see a **Recommended** section with suggestions based on what’s already in that list.

For the non-technical overview, see [Understand List Recommendations](/help/lists/Understand%20List%20Recommendations).

## What Recommendations Use as Input

Recommendations look for patterns in the titles you’ve added to the list, such as:

- Overlapping genres
- Shared themes/keywords
- Common cast (especially for movies)

WatchThis uses those signals to find candidate titles and rank them based on similarity and general quality signals (like rating and popularity).

## Why Recommendations Change Over Time

Recommendations refresh when the list changes (for example, you add or remove items), and they can also refresh periodically so results don’t get stale.

## Why You Might See No Recommendations

Common reasons include:

- The list is empty or too small to infer preferences
- The list is archived (recommendations are hidden)
- WatchThis doesn’t yet have enough information cached for the titles in your list to generate good matches

## Tips for Better Results

- Add a few titles that clearly match what you want the list to represent.
- Keep lists focused (a dedicated movies or TV list often yields clearer suggestions).
