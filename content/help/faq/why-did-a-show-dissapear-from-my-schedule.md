If a TV show was scheduled before but no longer shows up in your schedule prompts, it’s usually one of these reasons.

## 1) The Show Is Marked Completed or Dropped

WatchThis only schedules shows that are **Planning**, **Watching**, or **Paused**.

If a show becomes **Completed** (for example, you finished the latest aired episode) or you mark it **Dropped**, it won’t be eligible for scheduling, and scheduled entries may be removed.

Fix:

1. Open the show’s details.
2. Check your watch status.
3. Set it back to **Watching** or **Paused** if you still want to schedule it.

Read: [Watch Status Explained](/help/tracking/watch-status-explained).

## 2) You Already Watched an Episode Today

WatchThis can hide scheduled “Have you watched this today?” prompts if you’ve already watched an episode of that show today.

Fix:

- Check the show’s **Schedule** tab to confirm it’s still scheduled.
- Come back tomorrow (or on the next scheduled day).

Read: [Mark the Next Episode Watched](/help/tracking/mark-the-next-episode-watched).

## 3) The Show Isn’t Scheduled for Today (Timezone Mismatch)

Scheduled prompts are based on your timezone setting.

Fix:

1. Go to **Profile** (`/profile`).
2. Confirm your timezone is correct.

Read: [Edit Your Profile](/help/profile/edit-your-profile).

## 4) The Scheduled Day Was Removed (Including Sync)

If you removed the day, or you use syncing in a shared list and a collaborator’s schedule changes synced across, your schedule may change.

Fix:

1. Open the show → **Schedule** tab.
2. Re-add the days you want.

Read: [Weekly TV Schedule](/help/tracking/weekly-tv-schedule) and [How Sync Works in Shared Lists](/help/collaboration/how-sync-works-in-shared-lists).
